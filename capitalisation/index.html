Capitalisation

We minimise capitalisation to improve readability and come across as accessible, human and not overly formal. Capital letters make us seem self-important, legalistic and aloof.

Capitalisation should be limited to proper nouns and titles, including organisation names, job titles and official roles.

We minimise the use of proper nouns, only giving proper names to things where absolutely necessary.

Titles

Titles of books, publications and awards use title case.

The Veterinary Nursing Golden Jubilee Award

Nouns and proper nouns

Sometimes the same word, such as fellow, code or college, is used as both a noun and as a proper noun.

The RCVS offers a fellowship programme every year.

The RCVS offers a Fellowship programme every year.

Applicants for the RCVS Fellowship must be members of the College.

Applicants for the RCVS fellowship must be members of the college.

Janet Greene, RCVS Fellow, said that she was very pleased about the appointment.

Janet Greene, RCVS fellow, said that she was very pleased about the appointment.

My role at the College is very rewarding.

My role at the college is very rewarding.

The RCVS is one of the oldest royal colleges in the UK.

The RCVS is one of the oldest royal Colleges in the UK.

Headings

Headings and subheadings should always be in sentence case. This means only the first letter of the first word in a heading or title should be capitalised, unless it’s a proper noun.

The role of the RCVS

The Role of the RCVS

Importance

We don’t make things proper nouns in order to give them importance. This is always true of ordinary nouns and concepts, which we never capitalise, however important they are.

animal health

Animal Health

We award diplomas and certificates to veterinary surgeons.

We award Diplomas and Certificates to veterinary surgeons.

Our strategic plan for 2020-2024 sets out how we will be working to achieve this vision, and is built on a foundation of four key ambitions: clarity, compassion, courage and confidence.
