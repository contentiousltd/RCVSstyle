Lists

Lists are especially useful for web content. They help readers scan a page, can improve readability and help condense wordy sentences into clear, succinct points. well structured content can also improve search engine rankings. Lists can also be helpful for highlighting key details.

Here’s how we use them at the RCVS.

Bulleted or unordered lists

A list of key points, such as

Farm animals covered by the legislation include:

  • Cows
  • Sheep
  • Pigs

Numbered or ordered lists

A series of steps that should be followed in that order or a defined, complete set, for example:

Vets ensure the health and welfare of animals by maintaining five principles of practice:

  1. Professional competence
  2. Honesty and integrity
  3. Independence and impartiality
  4. Client confidentiality and trust
  5. Professional accountability

Punctuating lists

There are different ways to punctuate bulleted and numbered lists, depending on the substance. Always introduce the items in the list with a colon.

If the items in the list are fragments, start each item with a capital letter, but don’t put a full stop at the end.

Types of council members:

  • Members elected by the profession
  • Members appointed by vet schools
  • Appointed lay members
  • Appointed vet nurses 

If each item in the bulleted list is a full sentence, punctuate it like a sentence with a capital letter and a full stop.

How to apply for a fellowship:

  1. Write 2,000 words about your achievements.
  2. Tell us about your discipline and caseload.
  3. Share details of work that demonstrate your knowledge.

If the bulleted list itself could be replaced by one full sentence, punctuate it the same way you would without the bullet points: introduce the list with a colon, don’t capitalise the first word of each bullet, put a comma at the end of each item, and a comma and an and at the end of the penultimate item.

These recommendations are designed to:

  • enhance the role of vet nurses,
  • modernise RCVS registration, and
  • ensure the regulation of veterinary practices. 

When using bullet points, be consistent and make sure all the items in the list are in the same format.
